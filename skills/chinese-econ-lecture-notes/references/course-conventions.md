# Course Conventions

## Intermediate Microeconomics

Use a problem-solving structure:

1. Environment
2. Preferences or technology
3. Constraints
4. Optimization
5. Comparative statics
6. Intuition

Default notation:

- Utility: `u`, `U`
- Consumption bundle: `x`, `c`
- Prices: `p`
- Income or wealth: `m`, `w`
- Lagrangian: `\mathcal{L}`

When the lecture is graphical, explain the graph in prose even if the image is not reproduced.

## International Economics

Separate the model family clearly:

- Ricardian
- Heckscher-Ohlin
- Specific factors
- Open-economy macro

Default notation:

- Home and Foreign: `H`, `F`
- Exchange rate: define carefully because instructors vary between direct and indirect quotation
- Relative price and terms of trade: state the exact convention being used

When trade diagrams appear, prioritize assumptions, axes, and comparative statics over decorative explanation.

## Econometrics

Use a structure that makes assumptions visible:

1. Model setup
2. Estimator or identification strategy
3. Assumptions
4. Derivation or proof sketch
5. Interpretation
6. Pitfalls or diagnostics

Default notation:

- Sample size: `n`
- Estimator: `\hat{\beta}`
- Error term: `u`, `\varepsilon`
- Expectation and variance: `\E`, `\Var`
- Probability limit: `\plim`

If the lecture alternates between intuition and proof, preserve both instead of collapsing everything into formulas.

## Asset Pricing

Keep the pricing object explicit from the start:

- State prices
- Stochastic discount factor
- Euler equation
- No-arbitrage condition

Default notation:

- Return: `R`
- Excess return: `R^e`
- Pricing kernel or SDF: `m`
- Beta representation: define the factor and covariance terms explicitly

When the lecture is abstract, add a short intuition paragraph after each major equation.

## Cross-Course Rules

- Keep notation stable within one note, even if the lecture transcript is inconsistent.
- When the instructor changes symbols mid-lecture, pick one house notation and mention the mapping once.
- For Chinese notes, do not over-translate standard math notation. Translate the explanation, not the symbols.
